**Physical Foundations:**
- Transmon qubit physics: Josephson energy (EJ), charging energy (EC), anharmonicity (α = -EC), and the transmon regime where EJ/EC ≫ 1

**Design Constraints & Best Practices:**
- Qubit frequencies: Typically 4-6 GHz, with neighboring qubits detuned by >100 MHz to avoid crosstalk
- Readout resonator frequencies: 6-8 GHz, detuned from qubit by >1 GHz for dispersive readout
- Qubit-resonator coupling: g/2π typically 50-150 MHz for fast readout while maintaining coherence
- Resonator-feedline coupling: κ/2π = 1-10 MHz for multiplexed readout with κ < g < Δ hierarchy
- T1 Purcell limit: T1_max = (Δ/g)²/κ, requiring careful balance of detuning and coupling

**Topological & Frequency Planning:**
- Frequency collision avoidance: Use graph coloring to assign qubit frequencies such that coupled qubits have different colors
- Multiplexed readout: Group 4-8 qubits per feedline with resonator spacing ≥100 MHz
- Cross-resonance gates: Require frequency differences of 100-300 MHz between control and target qubits
- Two-qubit gate fidelity depends on: coupling strength (J/2π ~ 1-10 MHz), anharmonicity, and frequency detuning

**Critical Design Trade-offs:**
- Higher EJ/EC ratio: Improved charge noise immunity but reduced anharmonicity
- Stronger qubit-resonator coupling: Faster readout but increased Purcell decay
- Larger coupling capacitance: Higher g but more frequency crowding
- Tighter qubit spacing: Higher coupling but more crosstalk

When generating designs, prioritize: (1) frequency collision avoidance, (2) maintaining dispersive regime (g < Δ), (3) achievable coupling strengths for target gate operations, (4) coherence-limited performance (T1, T2), and (5) fabrication feasibility with realistic junction and capacitance values.
